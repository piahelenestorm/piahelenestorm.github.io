Healthy Together

A health + wellness app that helps motivate busy parents with small children to achieve a healthier everyday life. It contains a library of built-in workouts, mini-courses, recipes and much more.

The project

'Healthy Together' was a real-life project during the 6th semester of the bachelor 'Digital Concept Development', where we collaborated with UCL and the Steno Diabetes Center in Denmark to create a concept with the goal of helping and motivating busy parents with small children to achieve a healthier everyday life.

Project duration

April 2023 - June 2023

Client

UCL & Steno Diabetes Center

Team

4 UX designers

Skills

+ User Experience
+ Visual Design
+ Design System
+ Prototyping
+ Usability Testing

The problem

Parents with young children often have a very busy everyday life and therefore lack the resources and mental energy to prioritize their own health. As a result they have a tendency to end up with a liftestyle based on what is easiest and least time-consuming.

The problem with most health and wellness apps on the market today are that they target people who have a more flexible everyday life than parents with small children do.

"How might we help parents with young children to achieve a healthier everyday life?"

Conducting user research

We started the project by conducting user research among the target group to gain an understanding of what is keeping busy parents from living a healthy everyday life.

Questionnaire

First of all, we sent out a questionnaire to parents with young children about their daily health and what challenges they have in relation to this, as well as which functions they would like in a health app. There were 78 responses, 69 of which were women.

According to the respondents, one of major reasons why they opt out of the healthy choices in everyday life is that they feel they lack time and energy, and that they feel bad about having to spend time away from the children. The majority of the respondents expressed that they wanted access to quick and effective training routines, that they can perform at any time during the day as well as quick and healthy recipes.

Face-to-face interviews

To verify the findings from the questionnaire we carried out 5 semi-structured interviews with 2 women and 3 men from the target group. The purpose of the interviews were to elaborate on the struggles the parents face in relation to keeping a healthy lifestyle.

All interviewees wanted a healthier lifestyle, but generally lacked motivation to fulfil it. 4 out of the 5 would use a health app if it offered short and flexible training routines that could be performed at any time during the day. 3 out of 5 also said, that they would love to receive notifications to be reminded of working out and to take care of themselves.

"I never know what the day will bring, so I need access to an effective and flexible workout that I can do whenever there is time. Also, I need to be reminded of this, cause I am easily distracted when I'm with the baby." - a mom with small children

Personas

Based on the performed user research we created 2 personas representing the moms and dads from the target group. The moms generally expressed that they had a hard time prioritizing themselves and felt bad when doing so. At the same time many of the moms said they found it difficult "letting go of control" and letting the dad get more responsibility of taking care of the home and the kids.

Meanwhile, the dads generally said, that they found it difficult to understand how they could support the moms in the best way. They also had same wishes as the moms, of being better at prioritising their health in order to become a better partner and parent.

User Journey Map

To get an overview of the users' journey in relation to the app, we created a User Journey Map. Here, the users' journey is mapped from the initial decision-making proces, and shows their experiences and emotions along the journey.

While using the app there can be times where the users lack the time or motivation to keep following a workout, and therefore it is necessary that the users are reminded to keep prioritising themselves, otherwise it might result in them abandoning the app all together.

Designing a health + wellness app for busy parents

Based on the insights it was decided that the app should be built around a concept, where the parents can either pick and choose workouts from a built-in library or they can sign up for mini-courses that fits their current level of fitness and available time schedule.

Moreover they should have access to a library of recipes, that are all quick and easy to make. Lastly they should have access to a library of podcasts about parenthood and how to achieve a better health - both mentally and physical. Prior to starting the design, we created 3 design principles that summarizes the goal of the solution, and to guide us through the design proces:

"Keeping it simple. Motivating and gentle. User customization and flexible."

The app offers a library of built-in workouts and mini-courses, all divided according to available time, motivation and the level of fitness. Furthermore the mini-courses are divided into the categories 'Workouts', 'Diet' and 'Sleep' for a more holistic approach to health.

A key part of the solution is the use of nudging in the form of notifications. The users are reminded daily of the mini-courses they are a part of and are getting gentle reminders about how small steps can have a big effect on their health. The purpose of this is to gently motivate the parents, especially the moms, who had expressed many feelings of guilt concerning their health and taking time away from their kids.

Testing the solution

The prototype was tested by 5 parents from the target group (3 women and 2 men). The usabily tests was conducted as Concurrent Think Alouds, with the purpose of examining the participants feelings towards the app's concept and the usability of the app. The tests were conducted at the homes of the participants in the busiest hours of the day in order to test the usability of the app while in a semi-stressful environment.

60% of the participants were excited about the mini-courses and their flexibility, where the rest believed that they would primarily use the app to get inspiration for recipes and healthy food. The women all thought the content was appealing, however the men wanted access to content more related to them.

Although being a core function of the app, 2 out of 5 wanted the option to deny notifications entirely, as too many notifications would have a negative impact on their experience.

Conclusion

Based on the results from the usability tests, it was concluded that 'Healthy Together' could help busy parents achieve a healthier life. However, based on the wishes of the dads, there should be made some adjustments to make the app feel as welcoming to the dads.
