The Wardrobe

A wardrobe app giving the employees from the Royal Danish Ballet's school in Odense access to doing online searches among their 1000+ costumes from anywhere and at any time.

The project

'The Wardrobe' is an app created for the costume department at The Royal Danish Ballet's school in Odense, in which the employees can do online searches among their 1000+ costumes. Before the app was created, they didn't have an immediate way to get a clear overview of which costumes are in the wardrobe, and therefore needed a solution to access the costumes digitally.

Project duration

June 2023 - February 2024

Client

The Royal Danish Ballet's school (Odense)

Team

The problem

The school's creative staff often have meetings in the school's office, which is located on the top floor of the school. From here they discuss costumes that are located in the basement, and oftentimes an employee has to run up and down the stairs to go look for a costume. This can result in a lot of trips up and down the stairs during a meeting.

Therefore, the creative staff wants a digital solution where they can get a clear overview of all the costumes in the basement. Furthermore, the costume department depends on voluntary work, which is why the solution must be cheap and easy to maintain.

"How might we be able to access our 1000+ costumes at anytime and from anywhere?"

Conducting user research

In order to get an overview of how the school uses the costume department and what challenges they have, I did user research at the school.

Contextual Inquiry

I did contextual inquiry with the manager of the costume department, where she showed me around the costume room, while she explained her work processes. First of all, there weren't currently any system, besides color coding done by the leader of the costume department. Besides that, they depended on her memorizing which costumes they have, and with 1000+ costumes, it could tend to be a problem.

In addition, I conducted a semi-structured interview, where I asked the leader of the costume department how she imagined that they would use a digital solution. She wanted access to the wardrobe from anywhere in the building and from devices such as smartphones and laptops. She also wanted some kind of system in the basement with letters and numbers, to be able to find pieces of costumes at very specific places.

"We need to be able to sit in a meeting in the office at the top of the building and talk about costumes that are located in the basement, without having to go up and down the stairs over and over. Also, the costume room is a mess and it should be quick and easy to find a costume." - head of the costume department

Storyboarding

I created a storyboard to visualize the work processes regarding the costumes and to sketch ideas for the solution. The storyboard showcases the problems arising, when the staff members discuss the costumes at the office on the top of the building. Right now, they have to rely on their memory of which costumes are in the basement, or send a member down to check the room.

Designing a wardrobe app

Since the costumes were already sorted by color, I decided, together with the head of the costume department, that we would leave the clothes hanging as they did. Then we decided to create a system where the cases were given a number and the shelves were given a letter.

The solution had to be as cheap and easy to maintain as possible, which is why I decided in collaboration with the back-end developer on the team that the solution should be created as a PWA. In this way, there would be only one codebase to maintain and the solution could be accessed from all digital devices. Prior to starting the design I created 2 design principles that summarizes the goal of the solution, and to guide me through the design proces:

Searching on the go. Cheap and easy to manage.

Wireframes

To generate ideas for the solution's design, I sketched several wireframes. It was decided that the design's core function would be the search function. In addition, all employees should be able to add new costumes to the app with relevant tags and the placement of the costume in the costume room.

The final product

The solution was built upon a Realtime Database, where the 1000+ costumes would be stored with associated categories, such as the color of the clothes, size etc. When an employee wants to find a particular costume, they can press the picture to find the placement of the costume in the basement.

Testing the solution

I conducted a usability test among the head of the costume department and a volunteer to examine whether the solution was usable and user-friendly. They both got a set of photos of costumes that they themselves had to find in the costume room using only the app.

I followed them as they performed the tasks to see how they handled them. The usability tests were 100% succesful and they both completed the tasks without needing any assistence during. Also, thanks to the letters and numbers system, they were able to locate the costumes very quickly.

"That was really easy. When I got the photos of the costumes I had to find, I just put tags into the search field in the app, that I thought described the costume (like color or category) and then I found the location of the costume." - a volunteer from the costume department

What's next?

After delivering the app, I have helped getting started with adding costume items to the database, to see how well the app performs in real life. There were a few issues revolving the sizes of the images making the app a bit slow, but it was resolved by compressing the images when uploading them. Moving on from this, I will check back with the costume department after some time has went by, to get feedback from them about the performance of the app in their daily tasks and if further improvements needs to be made.
