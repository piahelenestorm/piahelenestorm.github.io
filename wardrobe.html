The Wardrobe is an online catalogue of the Royal Danish Ballet School Odense's 1000+ costumes.

Giving the employees and creatives access to their costume wardrobe from anywhere and at anytime.

Keeping track of an ever-growing wardrobe and with no overview of the costume room's content is hard.

The school's creative staff often have meetings in the school's office, which is located on the top floor of the school. From here they discuss costumes that are located in the basement, and oftentimes an employee has to run up and down the stairs to go look for a costume. This can result in a lot of trips up and down the stairs during a meeting. Therefore, the creative staff wants a digital solution where they can get a clear overview of all the costumes in the basement.

"How might we... be able to access our 1000+ costumes at anytime and from anywhere?"

To understand how the school uses the costume department and which challenges they have, I did user research, including a contextual inquiry and a semistructured interview with the head of the costume department. She gave me a tour of the costume room, while she explained her work processes. There weren't currently any organization system, besides basic color coding, and she currently were the only one from the staff who had an idea of which costumes where in the basement.

Designing a wardrobe app that contains an online catalogue of all the costumes in storage.

To visualize the problems arising when the employees had creative meetings about anything costume-related, and to generate ideas for the solution, I created a storyboard. It was clear from start, that what they really needed was a solution built around a search function, in which they could search for very specific items. Therefore I sketched several wireframes with a search function as the design's core, and with an idea for a way to organize the clothes in boxes and shelves labelled with letters and numbers.

The final product

Search using specific tags. Quickly locate in the costume room.

Testing the solution among the head of the costume department and a volunteer.

I conducted a usability test among the head of the costume department and a volunteer to examine whether the solution was usable and user-friendly. They both got a set of photos of costumes that they themselves had to find in the costume room using only the app. I followed them as they performed the tasks to see how they handled them. The usability tests were 100% succesful and they both completed the tasks without needing any assistence during. Also, thanks to the letters and numbers system, they were able to locate the costumes very quickly.

What's next?

After delivering the app, I have helped getting started with adding costume items to the database, to see how well the app performs in real life. There were a few issues revolving the sizes of the images making the app a bit slow, but it was resolved by compressing the images when uploading them. Moving on from this, I will check back with the costume department after some time has went by, to get feedback from them about the performance of the app in their daily tasks and if further improvements needs to be made.
