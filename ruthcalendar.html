Ruth Calendar

A unique and accessible digital planner experience, that can be tailored to fit the users' vision challenges and specific design needs. Designed in collaboration with low vision users.

The project

I'm co-founder of 'Ruth Calendar', a non-profit project with the mission of creating an acessible digital planner, that can be tailored to fit the users' vision challenges. It is created in close collaboration with low vision users, and is currently still under construction.

The team behind consists of 3 people, where I have worked primarily with UX research, interaction design and visual design. Along the way I also contributed with frontend development whenever possible. From the very beginning of the project the team has received alot of support from the organizations 'Instituttet for Blinde og Svagsynede' samt "nedsatsyn" (organizations for the visually impaired), whom have supported with the recruitment of test participants within the target group.

Accessible design can be...

beautiful useful pleasant life-changing

In the modern world it has become essential for many to plan and organize activities to create structure in their everyday life and to remember important events. However, people with low vision can experience severe challenges using digital planners due to insufficient text sizes and bad color contrasts.

For some, they experience so many problems that they completely give up keeping a planner themselves, and therefore become completely dependent on their relatives to keep track of their own lives. The goal of the project is therefore to create a solution that gives people with low vision their independence back.

"How might we help people with low vision in a way, so that they independently can organize and keep track of appointments and events in their daily life?

Conducting user research

According to The International Agency for the Prevention of Blindness (IAPB) approximately 253 million people globally live with impaired vision. Most often this is due to eye diseases such as cataract, glaucomas, age-related macular degeneration, diabetic retinopathy and retinitis pigmentosa. In addition, an unknown number of people live with temporarily impaired vision due to, among other things, stress, late effects from concussion and whiplash

Face-to-face-interview with IBOS

A face-to-face personal interview was held with Rikke and Ole from the Institute for the Blind and Visually Impaired in September 2022. Rikke works daily as team leader for the vision consultants who take on home visits to citizens with impaired vision, where Ole is IT expert within the work with accessible web solutions. The aim of the interview was to obtain qualitative knowledge through conversation with professionals who know a wide sample of the design needs that may arise with people with impaired vision. Furthermore, the interview had a purpose to uncover the challenges that vision consultants experiences when setting up digital devices at people with reduced vision, and what challenges they face may have during the use of a calendar i daily life

The Institute for the Blind and Visually Impaired works daily with visually impaired people who officially have less than 33% of their sight back, but they also work with people with late sequelae of concussion (PCS), who suffer from visual disturbances in everyday life. On mobiles and tablets they have limited settings available, and therefore they find that they often have to give thanks for a solution that comes so close on the citizen's needs as possible, but as if on none way is appropriate for them.

All interviewees expressed that they wanted a healthier lifestyle in general with more exercise and a healthier diet, but that at the same time they lacked motivation for this. 4 out of the 5 interviewees could see themselves using a health app in order to achieve a healthier life, but the general consensus was, that in order to achieve a healthier lifestyle, the app should offer effective and flexible training routines that could be performed at any time, considering the difficulty of planning the day ahead. The also said that they would love to get notifications to be motivated and reminded of working out.

DESIGNING THE CONCEPT

Based on the previous insights, it was decided that the app should be built around a concept, where the parents can either pick and choose workouts from a built-in library or they can sign up for mini-courses that fits their current level of fitness and available time schedule.

Moreover they should have access to a library of recipes, that are all quick and easy to make. Lastly they should have access to a library of podcasts about parenthood and how to achieve a better health - both mentally and physical. Prior to starting the design, we created 3 design principles that summarizes the goal of the solution, and to guide us through the design proces:

Keeping it simple. Motivating and gentle. User customization and flexible.

Customization and flexibility

The app offers a library of built-in workouts and mini-courses, all divided according to available time, motivation and the level of fitness. Furthermore the mini-courses are divided into the categories 'Workouts', 'Diet' and 'Sleep' for a more holistic approach to health.

Accessible themes doesn't have to be boring!

A key part of the solution is the use of nudging in the form of notifications. The users are reminded daily of the mini-courses they are a part of and are getting gentle messages about how small steps can have a big affect on their health. There is a big focus on the tone of voice in the app and a big part of that is the use of positive language. The purpose of this is to gently motivate the parents, especially the moms, who had expressed many feelings of guilt concerning their health and taking time away from their kids.

USABILITY TESTING

The prototype was tested by 5 parents from the target group (3 women and 2 men). The usabily test was conducted as a Concurrent Think Aloud, with the purpose of examining the participants feelings towards the app's concept and the usability of the app.

The test was held at the homes of the participants in the busiest hours of the day in order to test the usability of the app while in a semi-stressful environment. During the test the participants was asked to think aloud while performing 7 main tasks. The test administrator could ask follow-up questions during the task in case the participants said something that needed elaboration.

Der blev udført test over flere iterationer. 60% of the participants were excited about the option of signing up for mini-courses according to their level and available time, where the rest believed that they would primarily use the app to get inspiration for recipes and healthy food. In relation to the content, all the women thought the content sounded really exciting, where all the men wished they could access content more specific to the.

Although being a core function of the app, 2 of the 5 participants wanted there to be an option to opt out of notifications entirely, as this would have a negative impact on their experience.

CONCLUSION

Based on the results from the usability testing, it was concluded that an app build around the concept of mini-courses and the use of nudging could help busy parents with small children achieve a healthier life. However, based on the wishes of the dads, there should be made some adjustments to make the app feel as welcoming to the dads. This includes creating a section with special content targeted men and their specific needs and challenges in relation to their health.
