SUND SAMMEN

SUNDSAMMEN was a real-life project in the 6th semester of the bachelor 'Digital Concept Development'. As a part of a team of 4 people consisting of user researchers and designers we collborated with UCL and the Steno Diabetes Center in Denmark to create a concept with the goal of helping and motivating busy parents with small children to achieve a healthier everyday life. The concept was built around a health and wellness app with available mini-courses that the user could pick and choose from according to their schedule and motivation level.

In addition, the app offers podcasts about health and relationships as well as a recipe universe with easy and healthy recipes. A key part of the concept is the use of nudging to help motivate the users to stay on track on their chosen courses and to keep making the more healthy choice in the everyday life.

Project duration

April 2023 - June 2023

Client

UCL & Steno Diabetes Center

My role

User Experience, Visual Design, Design System, Prototyping, Usability Testing

THE PROBLEM

Parents of young children often have a very busy everyday life and therefore lack the resources and mental energy to prioritize their own health. As a result they have a tendency to end up with a liftestyle based on what is easiest and least time-consuming. At the same time, the healthcare system lacks the resources to be able to guide parents of young children on how to achieve better health after becoming parents. This can result in an increased risk of developing certain lifestyle diseases, such as diabetes, among this target group.

Most health and wellness apps on the market today are targeted a group who has a more flexible everyday life and who has more time to carry out fixed training programmes, than parents of small children do. Therefore, the goal was to develop a solution that focuses on improving the overall health of parents of small children, while also taking into account, that they have very busy and unpredictable everyday lives.

How might we help parents with young children to have a healthier everyday life?
