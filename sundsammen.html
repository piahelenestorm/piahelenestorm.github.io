SUND SAMMEN

SUNDSAMMEN was a real-life project in the 6th semester of the bachelor 'Digital Concept Development'. As a part of a team of 4 people consisting of user researchers and designers we collborated with UCL and the Steno Diabetes Center in Denmark to create a concept with the goal of helping and motivating busy parents with small children to achieve a healthier everyday life. The concept was built around a health and wellness app with available mini-courses that the user could pick and choose from according to their schedule and motivation level.

In addition, the app offers podcasts about health and relationships as well as a recipe universe with easy and healthy recipes. A key part of the concept is the use of nudging to help motivate the users to stay on track on their chosen courses and to keep making the more healthy choice in the everyday life.

THE PROBLEM

Parents with young children often have a very busy everyday life and therefore lack the resources and mental energy to prioritize their own health. As a result they have a tendency to end up with a liftestyle based on what is easiest and least time-consuming. At the same time, the healthcare system lacks the resources to be able to guide parents with young children on how to achieve better health after becoming parents. This can result in an increased risk of developing certain lifestyle diseases, such as diabetes, among this target group.

Most health and wellness apps on the market today are targeted a group who has a more flexible everyday life and who has more time to carry out fixed training programmes, than parents with small children do. Therefore, the goal was to develop a solution that focuses on improving the overall health of parents with small children, while also taking into account, that they have very busy and unpredictable everyday lives.

How might we help parents with young children to have a healthier everyday life?

USER RESEARCH

We started the project by conducting user research among the target group to gain an understanding of their pain points and their goals.

Questionnaire:

First of all, we sent out a questionnaire among parents with young children about their daily health and what challenges they have in relation to this, as well as what functions they would like in a health app. There were 78 responses, 69 of which were women.

According to the respondents, one of the biggest reasons why they opt out of the healthy choices in everyday life is that they feel they lack time and energy, and at the same time they feel bad about having to spend time away from the children. The majority of the respondents expressed that they would love to get access to quick and effective training routines, that they can perform at any time during the day as well as quick and healthy recipes.

Face-to-face interviews:

To verify the findings from the questionnaire we carried out 5 semi-structured interviews with 2 women and 3 men from the target group. The purpose of the interviews was to elaborate on the struggles the parents face in relation to keeping a healthy lifestyle.

All interviewees expressed that they wanted a healthier lifestyle in general with more exercise and a healthier diet, but that at the same time they lacked motivation for this. 4 out of the 5 interviewees could see themselves using a health app in order to achieve a healthier life, but the general consensus was, that in order to achieve a healthier lifestyle, the app should offer effective and flexible training routines that could be performed at any time, considering the difficulty of planning the day ahead. The also said that they would love to get notifications to be motivated and reminded of working out.

"I never know what the day will bring, so I need access to an effective and flexible workout that I can do whenever there is time. Also, I need to be reminded of this, cause I am easily distracted when I'm with the baby."

Personas

Based on the performed user research we created 2 personas representing the moms and dads from the target group. The moms generally expressed that they had a hard time prioritizing themselves and felt bad when doing so. At the same time many of the moms said they found it difficult "letting go of control" and letting the dad get more responsibility of taking care of the home and the kids.

Meanwhile, the dads generally said, that they found it difficult to understand how they could support the moms in the best way. They also had same wishes as the moms, of being better at prioritising their health in order to become a better partner and parent.

User Journey Map

To get an overview of the users' journey in relation to the app, we created a User Journey Map. Here, the users' journey is mapped from the initial decision-making proces, and shows their experiences and emotions along the journey.

It is expected that the users are made aware of the app though an indirect touchpoint such as social media, “word of mouth” from friends and family or from healthcare professionals. While using the app there can be times where the users lack the time or motivation to keep following a workout, and therefore it is necessary that the users are reminded to keep prioritising themselves, otherwise it might result in them abandoning the app all together.

DESIGNING THE CONCEPT

Based on the previous insights, it was decided that the app should be built around a concept, where the parents can either pick and choose workouts from a built-in library or they can sign up for mini-courses that fits their current level of fitness and available time schedule.

Moreover they should have access to a library of recipes, that are all quick and easy to make. Lastly they should have access to a library of podcasts about parenthood and how to achieve a better health - both mentally and physical. Prior to starting the design, we created 3 design principles that summarizes the goal of the solution, and to guide us through the design proces:

Keeping it simple. Motivating and gentle. User customization and flexible.

Customization and flexibility

The app offers a library of built-in workouts and mini-courses, all divided according to available time, motivation and the level of fitness. Furthermore the mini-courses are divided into the categories 'Workouts', 'Diet' and 'Sleep' for a more holistic approach to health.

Nudging and tone of voice

A key part of the solution is the use of nudging in the form of notifications. The users are reminded daily of the mini-courses they are a part of and are getting gentle messages about how small steps can have a big affect on their health.
